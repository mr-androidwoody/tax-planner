Cost basis seeding · gain ratio · ISA_ALLOWANCE cap · CGT exemption · cost basis reduction

Key engine facts: Cost basis is seeded as p1GIACost = p1Bal.GIA at the start of the projection, so gainRatio = 0 in year 1 — no CGT can arise from B&I in the first year regardless of transfer amount. Gain ratio = max(0, GIA − cost) / GIA, computed each year on the pre-growth, pre-B&I opening balance. B&I is capped at min(bniP1GIA, p1Bal.GIA, ISA_ALLOWANCE=£20,000). Cost basis reduces proportionally to the fraction transferred. CGT exemption = £3,000. CGT rates: 18% (basic band), 24% (higher band). All expected values hand-verified. Tolerance £1.00 for all engine tests. Multi-year tests use startYear=2026, endYear=2028 and read rows[1].
